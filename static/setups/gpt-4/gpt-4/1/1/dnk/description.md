The flag of Denmark, known as the Dannebrog, has a red field with a white Scandinavian cross. The cross design extends to the edges of the flag and is shifted to the hoist side.

Color codes:
- Red (Pantone 186 C): #C8102E
- White: #FFFFFF

Aspect ratio: 28:37

Geometric properties and measurements:
- The rectangle's width is divided into 28 units and its height into 37 units.
- The vertical bar of the cross is 6 units wide and placed 3 units from the hoist side (left edge).
- The horizontal bar of the cross is 6 units tall and placed at the center of the flag's height (18.5 units from top).

Other relevant information:
- The Danish flag is one of the oldest flags in continuous use, dating back to at least the 14th century.
- The flag's design is also referred to as a Nordic cross, which is a common feature among the flags of Nordic countries.
